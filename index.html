Recipes of the Old Gods

I feel like it's only fair to pay tribute to the old Norse god after whom this curriculum is named: Woden. The All-Father. Sometimes called the Hooded One or the Wanderer. No matter the name, he opened his Great Hall in Valhalla and, with the fallen warriors, feasts on the best game, endless mead, all the fruits of the world, breads, and cheeses. With the palate of a true king and the appetite of a bear, I think this website of recipes is quite fitting for the first project in The Odin Project.

With a little research and some digging through my grandmother’s oldest recipes, I came up with these three meals that I believe are fit for any god, king, warrior, or coder. Without further ado, here are my three choices.

Roast Boar of Everlasting Glory (Sæhrímnir)

In the sagas, the cook Andhrímnir slaughters the magical boar Sæhrímnir every night, and by morning it is whole again to feed Odin’s warriors.

Mead-Glazed Fowl of the Valkyries

Mead was the drink of the gods, flowing endlessly in Valhalla from the goat Heiðrún. To honor that, a bird roasted with mead glaze sounds fitting.

Hearth-Cheese & Honeyed Fruits of Idunn

The goddess Idunn guarded apples of immortality — fruit that gave the gods eternal youth. At Odin’s table, cheese and fruit would be served as symbols of plenty.
